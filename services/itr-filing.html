— WHO MUST FILE —

Income Tax Return is Mandatory For...

Whether you're a salaried employee, a business owner, or a company — if your income crosses the threshold, filing ITR is legally required. Here's who we serve:

Salaried Employees

Income from salary, one house property, interest income. Income above ₹2.5L (basic) or ₹5L (old regime) threshold mandates filing.

ITR-1 / ITR-2

Business Owners & Traders

Proprietors, traders, shopkeepers. If turnover exceeds ₹1 crore (business) or ₹50L (profession), audit is mandatory.

ITR-3 / ITR-4

Freelancers & Consultants

Doctors, lawyers, architects, IT professionals, designers — professional income requires ITR-3. Deduct business expenses legally.

ITR-3 / ITR-4

Private Limited Companies

All registered Pvt Ltd and Public companies must file ITR-6 mandatorily, regardless of profit or loss. Tax audit under 44AB required.

ITR-6

Partnership Firms & LLPs

All Partnership firms and LLPs must file ITR-5. Profit sharing among partners and capital accounts must be reported accurately.

ITR-5

Investors & HNIs

Income from mutual funds, stocks, real estate capital gains, FDs — all must be declared. Claim LTCG exemptions and set off losses.

ITR-2 / ITR-3

NRIs with India Income

NRIs with rental income, dividends, or capital gains from India must file. DTAA benefits available to avoid double taxation.

ITR-2

Trusts & NGOs

Charitable trusts, educational institutions, religious bodies registered under 12A/80G must file ITR-7 for continued tax exemption.

ITR-7

ITR FORM 1 — SAHAJ

Salaried Individuals

The simplest ITR form. Designed for resident individuals with straightforward income sources — salary, one house property, and small interest income.

  • Salaried employees with Form 16
  • Pensioners with pension income
  • Total income up to ₹50 lakhs only
  • Interest income from savings/FDs included
  • NOT for: business income, capital gains, foreign assets
Due Date: 31st July 2025 (AY 2025-26)

Documents Required

Keep these ready before we start — filing takes less than 2 hours once documents are in hand.

IDENTITY & BASIC
  • PAN Card
  • Aadhar Card (linked with PAN)
  • Bank account details (IFSC, account number)
INCOME PROOF
  • Form 16 Part A & Part B (from employer)
  • Salary slips for all months
  • Bank statements (all accounts)
  • Form 26AS / AIS from Income Tax portal

ITR FORM 4 — SUGAM

Small Business (Presumptive)

The easiest business ITR. For small businesses, traders, and professionals who opt for presumptive taxation — no detailed books needed.

  • Small shopkeepers, kirana store owners
  • Small contractors and sub-contractors (44AE)
  • Professionals with turnover ≤ ₹75L (44ADA)
  • Business with turnover ≤ ₹3 crore (44AD)
  • NOT for: Companies, LLPs, firms
Due Date: 31st July 2025 (AY 2025-26)

Documents Required

Presumptive taxation means no detailed books — just basic income and bank records.

BASIC IDENTITY
  • PAN Card and Aadhar Card
  • Bank account details (all used for business)
INCOME DETAILS
  • Total turnover / gross receipts for FY 2024-25
  • Bank statements (business + personal)
  • GST registration certificate (if registered)

ITR FORM 6

Corporate / Company

Comprehensive tax reporting for all companies registered in India (Pvt Ltd, Public Ltd, OPC) that do not claim exemption under Section 11.

  • Private & Public Limited Companies
  • One Person Companies (OPC)
  • Companies with business income or capital gains
  • Companies with foreign assets or income
Due Date: 31st Oct 2025 (Mandatory Audit)

Documents Required

Corporate filing is highly technical — we ensure alignment between ROC, GST, and Income Tax.

AUDITED RECORDS
  • Audited Financial Statements (P&L, Bal Sheet)
  • Tax Audit Report (Form 3CA/3CB-3CD)
  • MAT (Minimum Alternate Tax) calculations
  • Transfer Pricing report (if applicable)
COMPLIANCE ATTACHMENTS
  • ROC Filings (AOC-4, MGT-7)
  • Digital Signature (DSC) of Director
  • Details of Directors and Shareholding pattern
